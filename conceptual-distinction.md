# Conceptual Distinction

An identity-centered approach is conceptually distinct from a gateway. A
gateway may mediate access or traffic. Identity addresses the prior question of
whether the acting system should be treated as the governed system at all.

It is conceptually distinct from a scanner. A scanner may describe what is
present. Identity addresses whether what is present remains continuous with an
authorized entity.

It is conceptually distinct from a policy engine. A policy engine may evaluate
rules against facts. Identity addresses whether the subject of those rules is
still the subject that was authorized.

It is conceptually distinct from runtime governance alone. Runtime governance
may constrain activity in motion. Identity addresses the basis on which
permission to act should exist in the first place.

This repository does not explain how these distinctions are realized in
practice.
